    A Tambrahm Wedding!!

    • Welcome

      Now that (almost)everyone is here, the ceremony will begin.You will be listening to traditional music of nadaswaram (wind instrument) and melam (percussion) throughout the ceremony

    • Kashiyatra

      This is a fun tradition where the bride's father would have to entice the groom to marry his lovely daughter instead of leaving for pilgrimage Watch out for Shradda's dad to convince Karthik.

    • Maalai Matral (Garland Exchange)

      In an ancient Hindu tradition the garlanding signifies that Shradda is selecting Karthik as the man she wants to marry. As a fun tradition, the couple's uncle will lift on to their shoulders and they will have to compete to garland the other first.

    • Kanyathanam (Giving away the bride)

      Shradda will be seated on her Dad's lap, while he performs a ritual to symbolize a gift of his daughter to her new family

    • Mangalaya Dharanam (Tying of sacred thread)

      Karthik will tie the Thali (equivalent to a wedding ring) around Shradda's neck. karthik's sister (Charanya/Krithika) will strengthen the knot by tying a final knot. They are now married. Elders will use rice to bless the couple with a traditional blessing which translates to: "May you live and enjoy a hundred autmns together."

    • Sapta Padi (Seven Steps Around The Sacred Fire)

      To nourish each other.

      To grow together in strength.

      To preserve our wealth.

      To share our joys & sorrows.

      To care for our Children & Parents.

      To be together forever.

      To remain friends, lifeling.
